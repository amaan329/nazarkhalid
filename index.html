About Me

I am a PhD student in Demography & Population Studies at the University of Pennsylvania, where I was honored to receive the Inaugural Presidential PhD Fellowship. My academic journey began with a degree in Economics from Hindu College, University of Delhi, followed by an MA and MPhil in Economics from Jawaharlal Nehru University, New Delhi. I grew up in the Indian states of Bihar and Maharashtra.

At Penn, I am also a Research Fellow at r.i.c.e, where I lead projects studying discrimination, sanitation behavior, and barriers to clean fuel adoption. My goal is to combine rigorous quantitative research with meaningful insights that address inequalities in South Asia and beyond.

Research Interests

  • Health disparities in South Asia
  • Gender, caste, and religious discrimination
  • Fertility, mortality, and population dynamics
  • Household survey methods
  • Sanitation and environmental health

Research

My research focuses on how social inequalities — across gender, caste, and religion — shape health outcomes and demographic patterns. I have led a phone survey measuring discrimination against women, Dalits, and Muslims, as well as a large household panel survey on sanitation behavior. I am also studying the barriers households face in adopting clean fuel despite its clear health benefits.
