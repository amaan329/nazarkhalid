About

Nazar Khalid is a doctoral student in Demography and Sociology at the University of Pennsylvania. His research focuses on family demography, fertility, and health with an emphasis on South Asia. Nazar is particularly interested in intergenerational relationships and the role of family structure in shaping well-being across the life course.

He holds a Master’s in Population Studies from the International Institute for Population Sciences (IIPS), Mumbai, and a BA in Social Sciences from Tata Institute of Social Sciences (TISS), Hyderabad.

Research

Nazar’s work has explored issues such as fertility decline, family planning, parental involvement in education, and the intersections of health and social policy in South Asia. His ongoing research also examines demographic changes and their implications for intergenerational support and caregiving.
