About Me

I am a PhD student in Demography & Population Studies at the University of Pennsylvania, honored with the Inaugural Presidential PhD Fellowship. I earned my B.A. in Economics from Delhi University, and my M.A. & M.Phil. in Economics from Jawaharlal Nehru University, New Delhi. Originally from Bihar and Maharashtra, my research is grounded in South Asian contexts.

I am also a Research Fellow at r.i.c.e Institute, leading projects on sanitation behavior, social inequality, and barriers to clean fuel adoption.

Research Interests

  • Social inequality across gender, caste, and religion
  • Household sanitation and environmental health
  • Fertility, mortality, and population dynamics
  • Quantitative survey methods
  • Social policy in South Asia

Research Projects

My research blends demographic analysis with social perspectives to explore how inequality and environmental factors shape health, education, and behavior. I study:

  • Discrimination in sanitation behavior and mental health
  • Persistence of solid fuel use despite LPG subsidies
  • Impacts of floods on children's learning in rural India
  • Discrimination using phone surveys among marginalized groups
