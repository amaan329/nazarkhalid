About

Nazar Khalid is a PhD student in Demography & Population Studies at the University of Pennsylvania. He was awarded the Inaugural Presidential PhD Fellowship in recognition of his outstanding research potential.

He holds an MA and MPhil in Economics from Jawaharlal Nehru University, New Delhi, and earned his undergraduate degree in Economics from Hindu College, University of Delhi. He grew up in the Indian states of Bihar and Maharashtra.

At Penn, Nazar is a Research Fellow at r.i.c.e, where he has led a phone survey measuring discrimination against women, Dalits, and Muslims, as well as a household panel survey on sanitation behavior and research examining barriers to clean fuel adoption — despite its health benefits.

Research Interests

  • Family demography and intergenerational relationships
  • Fertility transitions and family planning in South Asia
  • Education and child well-being
  • Health, aging, and caregiving dynamics
  • Population change and social policy

Research

Nazar's research examines demographic processes, fertility decisions, and the consequences of population change. His work combines quantitative and qualitative methods to understand family, health, and education outcomes in South Asia and beyond.
