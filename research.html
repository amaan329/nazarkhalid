Climate & Education Infrastructure India 2025

Floods, community infrastructure, and children’s learning losses in rural India

Khalid, N., Behrman, J., Hannum, E., & Thapa, A. — Economics of Education Review (2025)

Summary: Flood exposure lowers children’s test scores, but losses are smaller in villages with stronger community infrastructure (roads, accessible schools, communal facilities). Infrastructure acts as a local shock absorber for education.

More details▸
Quasi-experimental estimates compare cohorts across flood intensities with community/household controls. Protective effects are largest for disadvantaged groups, implying resilience investments can narrow post-disaster learning gaps.

Climate & Education Inequality India 2024

Floods and children’s education in rural India

Khalid, N., Behrman, J., Hannum, E., & Thapa, A. — RSF Journal (2024)

Summary: Floods disrupt attendance and achievement; the harm is concentrated among marginalized households (by caste, religion, parental education). Resources and community support accelerate recovery for some children.

More details▸
Uses microdata to quantify heterogeneous effects and layered vulnerability. Policy implication: pair disaster response with targeted education support in flood-prone districts.

Energy & Gender Rural India 2021

Gender, government intervention, and slow LPG adoption in rural north India

Vyas, S., Gupta, A., & Khalid, N. — World Development (2021)

Summary: Subsidies expand LPG access, yet solid fuels persist. Gendered decision power, recurring refill costs, and supply reliability explain slow transition—connections alone don’t change usage.

More details▸
Recommends interventions that address intra-household bargaining and affordability, alongside last-mile supply.
