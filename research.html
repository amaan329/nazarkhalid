Floods, community infrastructure, and children’s learning losses in rural India

Economics of Education Review (2025) — Khalid, Behrman, Hannum & Thapa

Flood exposure reduces test scores; villages with stronger local infrastructure experience smaller learning losses — infrastructure can buffer climate shocks in education.

More details

Quasi-experimental estimates, heterogeneous effects by socio-economic groups; policy implication: invest in resilient school and community assets.

Floods and children’s education in rural India

RSF (2024)

Floods disrupt attendance and learning, with the worst effects on marginalized children. Recovery depends on household and community resources.

Uses IHDS microdata and robustness checks; suggests targeting education recovery programs in flood-prone, disadvantaged districts.
