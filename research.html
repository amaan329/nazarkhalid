Climate & Education Infrastructure India 2025

Floods, community infrastructure, and children’s learning losses in rural India

Khalid, N., Behrman, J., Hannum, E., & Thapa, A. — Economics of Education Review (2025)

Summary: The paper links flood exposure to measurable drops in test scores in rural India and shows that villages with stronger community infrastructure (e.g., accessible schools, roads, basic facilities) experience smaller learning losses. The core message is simple: local assets buffer climate shocks in education.

More details▸
Evidence is built by comparing children across different flood intensities while holding constant household/community characteristics. Effects appear larger for socio‑economically disadvantaged groups, underscoring infrastructure as an equity tool in climate adaptation.
